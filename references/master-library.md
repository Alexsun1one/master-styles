# Master Library

25 world-class masters, decomposed into transferable tokens. Each entry: identity → key
tokens → use when → restraint (the refusal) → transfer (anti-pastiche guard). Apply the
rule, never the picture. Choose by the token you need — see `blending.md`.

---

## A. Poster / Graphic / Editorial Design

### Josef Müller-Brockmann — the Swiss grid as moral order
- Palette: one or two flats + black/white; a single saturated field (often red) carries all energy on neutral ground. Color is structural.
- Composition: a modular mathematical grid IS the work; asymmetric, every element snapped to columns/baselines.
- Type: one sans-serif (Helvetica lineage), flush-left, locked to grid, restrained scale. Large active whitespace.
- Use when: authority, trust, institutional/technical seriousness; premium-through-discipline.
- Restraint: refuses decoration, illustration, and any unjustified placement.
- Transfer: build a real grid for the subject, snap content to it, ONE accent on neutral ground. Don't copy his concentric-circle motifs.

### A.M. Cassandre — the heroic Art Deco poster
- Palette: cool mechanical steely tones + one contrasting red; high contrast, no naturalistic shading; airbrush-smooth metal gradients.
- Composition: monumental, often symmetric, with extreme forced UPWARD perspective thrusting a hero object at the viewer; hairline speed-lines.
- Shape: objects abstracted to cones, cylinders, arcs. Type: all-caps Deco faces built INTO the scene as architecture.
- Use when: aspirational travel, transport, luxury, speed, heroic scale.
- Restraint: refuses fussy detail, ornament, lowercase.
- Transfer: pick the subject's hero object, low dramatic angle, primitive geometry, all-caps headline woven in. Abstract your own object — not his liners/trains.

### Saul Bass — one charged symbol in cut paper
- Palette: few flats, high contrast, one hot color (red/orange) on black or white; color is symbolic.
- Shape/texture: hand-scissored cut-paper silhouettes with subtly jagged imperfect edges; visible human hand. Maximal empty field around one symbol.
- Type: hand-cut, jagged, irregular lettering with tension.
- Use when: a single bold concept-symbol — titles, events, thrillers, anything psychological/noir.
- Restraint: refuses literal scene-depiction and clutter — one idea, simplest symbol.
- Transfer: find the ONE symbol for the subject, cut from paper, big empty field, one hot color. Invent your own symbol — not his arm/spiral/eye.

### Paula Scher — typographic maximalism, words as architecture
- Palette: bold, punchy, can clash on purpose; driven by impact.
- Composition: dense overlapping "shouting" layouts; type pushes, tilts, fills edge-to-edge; Constructivist angular dynamism.
- Type: eclectic mixed weights/faces, condensed bold sans set huge and tilted, words stacked into shapes.
- Use when: voice, urgency, cultural energy, theater, music, protest, youth.
- Restraint: refuses politeness, quiet hierarchy, and the rigid grid — disciplined through composition.
- Transfer: make words the entire image — huge headline, mixed weights/angles, overlapped to fill the frame. Build your own type system — not the Public Theater marks.

### Massimo Vignelli — timeless modernist absolutism
- Palette: limited; flat color used as a coding SYSTEM (each line/category a color) on neutral ground; functional, never mood.
- Composition: the grid is everything; diagrammatic abstraction over literal accuracy; strict 45°/90° angle system.
- Type: Helvetica above a tiny canon (Bodoni, Century, Futura); flush, on grid, timeless.
- Use when: information, wayfinding, identity systems; must feel permanent and trustworthy.
- Restraint: refuses trend, decoration, gratuitous typefaces.
- Transfer: strict grid + 45°/90° logic, one canon face, flat color as a code. Apply the diagrammatic method to your data — don't redraw the subway map.

### Stefan Sagmeister — type built from real physical material
- Texture (core): headlines literally constructed from objects — coins, fruit, thread, dollar bills; material IS the message.
- Light: real photographic light, shadow, depth, imperfection (a counter to flat digital design).
- Composition: staged like a photo of a made object; follows the idea/"trick".
- Use when: personal, provocative, manifesto, culture; benefits from surprise and a strong single idea.
- Restraint: refuses slick purely-digital design; the concept must justify the object.
- Transfer: find a material pun for the subject, build the headline physically from a relevant material, photograph for true light. Not his banana wall or skin-carving.

## B. Illustration

### Malika Favre — bold sensual minimalism, negative space IS the drawing  *(Pop/Op, NOT Art Deco)*
- Palette: two flat saturated colors in deliberate TENSION + black or white ground; signature red+blue clash.
- Composition: tight, centered/symmetric; bodies cropped so figure and ground interlock; Op-art banded rhythm.
- Negative space: maximal — large unbroken fields where ABSENCE defines the subject. Light as flat graphic bands (blind-slats), never gradients.
- Use when: confident sensuality, glamour, heat, a single charged mood — covers, beauty/fashion, luxe-graphic.
- Restraint: refuses gradients, modeling, clutter.
- Transfer: two clashing flats + negative space defining the subject + flat banded light, on any object. Choose your own clash pair — not her reclining-nude compositions.

### Noma Bar — one image hides a second in its negative space
- Palette: 2–4 flats, no blending; one figure-color + one ground-color so the gap reads as a hidden image.
- Motif (signature): dual-meaning negative space — the empty area engineered into a meaningful second shape. Single centered icon-like form, poster-frontal, flat, no shadow.
- Use when: a single witty idea needs an intelligent punch — editorial, op-ed, book covers.
- Restraint: refuses detail/shadow that doesn't serve the concept; refuses to state the second meaning.
- Transfer: design one silhouette whose gap forms a second relevant shape tied to the content. Don't reuse his catalog.

### Christoph Niemann — conceptual minimalism, the punch line in what you don't see
- Palette: minimal, near-monochrome or a few flats; color subordinate to idea.
- Shape: spare ink lines + flat shapes, deliberately "jotted" (actually heavily edited). Generous empty space framing one move.
- Motif: visual metaphor by substitution / scale-play — a mundane object reinterpreted as something else.
- Use when: a complex/abstract idea must land instantly with warmth — explainers, tech/culture editorial.
- Restraint: "the most important decision is what you leave out".
- Transfer: take ONE object from the subject's world, find the unexpected shape it becomes, add the fewest marks to complete it.

### Tom Haugomat — cinematic flat scenes, tiny faceless humans in vast quiet
- Palette: reduced two-tone / limited pastel; contrast (light vs dark) is the driver, not hue variety.
- Composition: cinematic wide / bird's-eye framing; deliberate scale contrast — a tiny figure dwarfed by a large environment; generous calm fields.
- Light (core): flat light/shadow blocking, high contrast, light as a spotlight carrying emotion. Faceless figures — feeling via body language and scale.
- Use when: atmosphere, solitude, contemplation, awe — travel, nature, literary covers.
- Restraint: refuses facial expression and detail clutter.
- Transfer: wide/bird's-eye frame, a small faceless figure dwarfed by a flat two-tone environment, one block of directional light. Remove faces.

### Olimpia Zagnoli — joyful pop, fluid rounded saturated shapes
- Palette: saturated vibrant polychrome (many combos tested), flat blocks; color is each work's identity.
- Shape (signature): soft fluid rounded organic curves (vs the hard edges of Favre/Bar); bold silhouettes read instantly. Flat, no naturalistic shadow.
- Use when: happy, warm, modern, friendly, pop — covers, packaging, energetic editorial.
- Restraint: refuses complexity and noise.
- Transfer: reduce the subject to soft rounded silhouettes, fill with a tested high-saturation palette, keep it flat and buoyant. Pick your own combo — not her face/sunglasses icons.

### Geoff McFetridge — "logos for human emotion", calm reductive figures
- Palette: limited, muted, quiet — grays, blues, whites, pinks on flat grounds; matte finish.
- Shape: flat color planes + clean contour lines + geometric reduction; faceless simplified figures reading as universal. Generous calm fields.
- Use when: calm, contemplative, understated modern — mindfulness, lifestyle, quiet-confidence brands.
- Restraint: refuses detail, individuality, loudness — emotion via reduction.
- Transfer: muted near-tonal palette, matte flat planes, anonymous faceless figures doing one simple gesture in a calm field.

## C. Fine Art / Painting (color, mood & composition tokens)

### Mark Rothko — Color Field, glowing soft-edged blocks as emotional weather
- Palette: a FIELD system — one dominant ground + 2–3 stacked rectangles in close values, built from many thin translucent glazes; color emanates from within.
- Composition: vertical; centered stacking of horizontal blocks with a small margin so they float; no horizon, no object. Internal glow, no shadow.
- Use when: mood, emotion, contemplation — abstract backgrounds, meditative covers.
- Restraint: refuses line, edge, gesture, depicted object — only color hovering.
- Transfer: 2–3 stacked soft-edged bands with feathered borders over a glowing ground; one hue family, pushed value; type in the calm margin. Invent fresh palette/proportions — not his Seagram blocks.

### Piet Mondrian — Neoplasticism, white field carved by black orthogonals
- Palette: only primaries + white/grey/black; color rationed to a few blocks; black is structure.
- Composition: asymmetric grid of black horizontals/verticals, rectangles of UNEQUAL size; balance by weight, never symmetry. Hard edges, flat, no depth.
- Use when: rational, modernist, structured, iconic-graphic.
- Restraint: refuses the diagonal, the curve, secondary colors, any depth.
- Transfer: asymmetric black-line grid with unequal rectangles, fill only 2–3 cells with saturated blocks, rest white. Vary line weight and which cells get color — don't clone the actual Composition.

### David Hockney — flat California sunlight
- Palette: bright saturated high-key, sun-bleached but clean — pool turquoise, sky blue, pink/terracotta, white; no mud.
- Composition: flat near-symmetric geometric planes, strong horizontals; one asymmetric event (a splash) against calm planes. Flat even daylight, minimal shadow.
- Shape: flat color shapes; water/textures as STYLIZED marks (squiggles, jigsaw patches), not realistic.
- Use when: sunny, clean, modern, still, optimistic — lifestyle, leisure, bright editorial.
- Restraint: refuses atmospheric depth, gloom, weather.
- Transfer: a few flat saturated planes in high-key sun palette + ONE stylized patterned incident; minimal shadow. Not a pool + splash redraw.

### Henri Matisse (cut-outs) — drawing with scissors
- Palette: pure saturated FLAT hues, no shading; bold contrasting blocks on white; each shape one uncompromised color.
- Shape: organic cut shapes where the cut edge IS the line; rhythmic all-over decorative balance; white ground breathes between shapes.
- Use when: joyful, decorative, bold, organic, timeless-modern — covers, stickers, lively cards.
- Restraint: refuses outline-drawing, modeling, perspective, blends.
- Transfer: flat organic paper-cut silhouettes in a few pure colors on white, rhythmic spacing, active negative space. Cut shapes for your subject — not his algae/Icarus.

### Gustav Klimt (golden phase) — flat gold ground packed with ornament
- Palette: gold dominant as ground/atmosphere + jewel-tone ornamental accents; gold = light and sacredness.
- Composition: flat, frontal, no perspective, figures pressed to the plane. Two-register contrast: naturalistic skin vs flat obsessively patterned surroundings. Maximalist ornament fill.
- Texture: gold/silver leaf, gesso relief, mosaic tessellation.
- Use when: premium, sacred, decorative, opulent.
- Restraint: refuses spatial depth and naturalistic background — flattens all but face/hands into ornament.
- Transfer: subject on a flat gilded ground, dense geometric+floral ornament, ONE element stays realistic against the pattern. Invent your own ornament — not The Kiss.

### Hokusai / Ukiyo-e — bold key-line + flat color + scale contrast
- Palette: flat pure bright zones bounded by line; limited palette; the Prussian-blue bokashi gradation supplies depth/mood within flat printing.
- Composition: daring asymmetry, unconventional viewpoint, strong diagonal/curve; massive foreground vs tiny distant anchor. Calligraphic empty areas (ma) balancing one dense form.
- Shape: a black key-block line outlines and breaks up flat color; no modeling, no shadow.
- Use when: dynamic, natural, dramatic, East-Asian-resonant, graphic.
- Restraint: refuses volumetric shading and Western chiaroscuro.
- Transfer: bold black contour + flat bright fills + one strong diagonal + dramatic scale contrast + a single graded-blue passage. Your own motif — not the wave.

### Edward Hopper — raking light, solitude, theatrical emptiness
- Palette: restrained muted tones + a few bold accents; warm sunlit planes vs cool shadow; light is the subject.
- Composition: strong architectural geometry, horizontal emphasis; cropped, off-center, voyeuristic framing; generous emptiness — bare walls, vacant rooms.
- Light (signature): low-angle raking light, long hard-edged shadows, strong contrast; light isolates.
- Use when: quiet, cinematic, melancholic, still subjects.
- Restraint: refuses clutter, crowd, incident — architecture + light + at most one lone figure.
- Transfer: horizontal architectural geometry + low raking light + long shadows + muted palette with one warm accent + empty space + one lone figure. Not a Nighthawks copy.

## D. Photography / Portrait

### Platon — a face filling the frame on a void
- Background: a non-color — blown-out white OR dead black OR a bare wall; the subject is the only event.
- Composition: extreme tight crop (often clips crown/chin), centered, frontal, slightly low so the subject reads monumental.
- Light (key): single source directly above the lens → butterfly light with a small shadow under the nose; wide-angle proximity, camera close, slight intended distortion. Forensic skin detail.
- Use when: a single face must carry authority and intimacy with direct address.
- Restraint: refuses environment, props, color seduction — anything that lets the eye leave the face.
- Transfer: subject on a true void, crop to clip the crown, one soft source just above the lens, shoot close/slightly-low, eyes centered. Change the lighting ratio to make it yours — not his Putin/Obama framing. *(Ideal for ranking-card portraits.)*

### Irving Penn — studio elegance through corner compression
- Background: neutral seamless — plain grey/off-white; near-tonal palette; matte long-toned (platinum-print) feel.
- Composition (signature): the Corner — two flats meeting at a sharper-than-90° angle, the sitter wedged in and compressed; otherwise centered, frontal, at most one prop. High emptiness around the figure.
- Light (key): single directional source mimicking north light from one side → gentle clear falloff. Exquisite material/skin texture.
- Use when: quiet gallery-grade elegance; a figure to feel composed, contained, timeless.
- Restraint: refuses theatrical lighting, busy sets, illusionistic environments.
- Transfer: converging-wedge framing gently boxing the subject + single soft side-light + flat neutral ground + one dominant texture. Keep the print matte and long-toned.

### Annie Leibovitz — cinematic conceptual environment
- Palette: bold rich saturated color as a mood instrument (controlled B&W for serious work).
- Composition: environmental, conceptual staging where the SET reveals character; symbolic/theatrical scenarios; dense designed environments (opposite of a void).
- Light (key): soft directional sculptural light placed in the direction ambient already travels → painterly chiaroscuro that reads natural but heightened; surroundings fall dark to spotlight the subject.
- Use when: the image should feel like a movie still — narrative, world-building, glamour.
- Restraint: refuses the empty void and the literal snapshot; light must stay motivated.
- Transfer: motivated soft key along the ambient direction + rich saturated grade + a constructed story-environment, on a new subject. Change the story, keep the grammar — not her famous restages.

## E. Asian / Japanese Design

### Kenya Hara / MUJI — emptiness as strategy
- Palette: near-white as the matrix + at most one restrained ink tone; white = potential, not background.
- Composition: a vast void with one small subject or line of type anchored in wide *ma* (the charged interval); the empty area IS the subject. Flat diffuse shadowless light.
- Texture: matte uncoated paper, tactile natural materials. Type small, quiet, generously leaded.
- Use when: calm, premium, contemplative, "essential" — quiet covers, breathing-room luxury.
- Restraint: refuses to fill, decorate, or impose meaning — leaves room for the viewer.
- Transfer: one small focal element in a near-white field + one ink tone, push 80%+ to empty ma, matte cues, tiny quiet type.

### Ikko Tanaka — tradition rebuilt from Bauhaus geometry
- Palette: limited flat saturated blocks (red, blue, yellow, black) on a soft neutral ground; color does the representing (red circles = makeup).
- Shape: pure geometry — circles, half-circles, squares, arcs as flat color blocks on a rigid grid; subject assembled from modular units (a geisha from squares + half-circles). Flat, form via edge and color contrast.
- Type: clean, legible, justified, set quietly in a corner.
- Use when: a cultural/traditional subject the brief wants modern, intelligent, iconic.
- Restraint: refuses illustration — constructs the subject from primitives, never draws it.
- Transfer: ask "which 5–7 flat geometric shapes signify this subject?", build on a grid in 3–4 flat colors on a soft ground, one quiet corner type block. Not the geisha redraw.

### Tadanori Yokoo — psychedelic maximalist collage
- Palette: vivid clashing intentionally garish — hot reds, golds, deep blues, pinks at full saturation; "more and louder".
- Composition: dense layered centralized drama, often radial — a sunburst of rays from a rising sun; theatrical frontal staging. Maximal density (the direct inverse of Hara).
- Shape/texture: hard silhouettes + cut-out collage edges + ray-stripes; silkscreen flatness with embedded photo fragments; woodblock references. Type jammed in as collage.
- Use when: energetic, retro, subversive, festival/music/theatrical; must grab attention loudly.
- Restraint: refuses clean modernism, emptiness, good-taste minimalism — coherence is conceptual, not quiet.
- Transfer: a central radial sunburst of clashing rays + dense layered subject-relevant collage + silkscreen-flat color + type in the chaos. Aim the maximalism at your subject's iconography — not his rising-sun self-portrait.

## F. Chinese / East-Asian Ink Aesthetics

The default register for 古代诗人 / 词人 / 文人 / 书画家 / 古典中国题材. Western masters do not
fit these — route here instead.

### 宋元山水 / Song–Yuan Landscape — monumental ink space
- Palette: ink monochrome (焦/浓/重/淡/清 — five ink tones) on warm rice paper; color absent or one faint mineral wash.
- Composition: vertical hanging-scroll; layered distance (高远/深远/平远); a small human dwarfed by mountains; mist as the connective void.
- Negative space (signature): 留白 / 计白当黑 — emptiness is active substance (cloud, water, sky), not background. Often half the scroll.
- Brush: 皴法 texture strokes for rock; a living, varied brush line; no outline-fill flatness.
- Use when: grandeur, contemplation, lineage, timelessness — poets, philosophers, classical subjects.
- Restraint: refuses saturated color, full frames, and Western perspective/shadow.
- Transfer: ink-tone range on rice paper, vertical layered depth, dominant 留白, one small focal subject. Build your own scene; don't copy 《富春山居图》.

### 文人画 / Literati Painting — the scholar's restraint
- Palette: sparse ink + occasional one accent (a fan of red, a touch of indigo); 逸 (untrammeled) over polish.
- Composition: asymmetric, off-center, much empty paper; painting + 题跋 inscription + 印章 seal as one integrated whole.
- Shape & line: economy of brush — a few decisive strokes imply the whole (a branch, a bird, a bamboo).
- Motif: the red 印章 (seal) as the single warm punctuation in an ink field; calligraphic inscription as part of the image.
- Use when: refined, scholarly, understated, personal — exactly the register for a poet ranking.
- Restraint: refuses density, decoration, and showing-off; 简 (sparseness) is the virtue.
- Transfer: a few decisive ink strokes, vast paper, a calligraphic label, one red seal accent. Invent your own subject — don't reproduce 八大山人's fish/birds.

### 书法 / Calligraphy — the line as expression
- Palette: black ink on paper; one red seal.
- Form: the brushstroke carries energy (筋骨血肉); 楷/行/草 registers from formal to wild; vertical columns, right-to-left.
- Use when: tier labels, titles, name plates in any ink-register card — 神品/逸品/妙品/能品 set in brush type read far better than S/A/B/C here.
- Restraint: refuses mechanical even weight; every stroke varies in pressure and speed.
- Transfer: set headline/tier labels as vertical brush calligraphy; let stroke weight vary; pair with a quiet 宋体 for body text.

### 工笔 / Gongbi — fine-line court painting
- Palette: fine outline + flat layered mineral pigments (石青/石绿/朱砂) + gold; controlled, jewel-like.
- Composition: refined, decorative, often on silk; flat space, meticulous detail.
- Use when: 帝王将相 / 仕女 / classical figures needing color and grandeur rather than sparse ink.
- Restraint: refuses loose gesture — precision and patience.
- Transfer: crisp fine outlines + flat mineral color + gold accents on a silk ground; one register across all figures.
